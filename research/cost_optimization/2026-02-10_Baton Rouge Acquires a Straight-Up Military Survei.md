## Summary

Baton Rouge Police Department has acquired a military-grade surveillance drone originally designed for war zones, expanding local law enforcement's ability to conduct mass aerial surveillance of civilian populations. This represents a direct militarization of policing technology with unprecedented tracking capabilities.

## Content

The Baton Rouge Police Department announced this week that it will begin using a drone designed by military equipment manufacturer Lockheed Martin and Edge Autonomy, making it one of the first local police departments to use an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) with a history of primary use in foreign war zones. Baton Rouge bought the Stalker VXE30 from Edge Autonomy, which partners with Lockheed Martin and began operating under the brand Redwire this week. According to reporting from WBRZ ABC2 in Louisiana, the drone, training, and batteries, cost about $1 million.
